01 — About me

A little bit of everything

Hi! I'm Sneha — a third-year ECE student at Northeastern University who gets equally excited about soldering an instrumentation amplifier and deploying Kotlin code on Android hardware.

My work lives at the edge where hardware meets software: designing CAN bus circuitry for a lunar rover one day, building Android features at Google the next. I love making things that are physically real — systems you can hold, wear, or watch excavate simulated moon regolith.

When I'm not in the lab, you might find me at the NU Beekeeping Society, running SWE events, or teaching kids to code robots at a summer camp. I believe in making engineering joyful and accessible.
